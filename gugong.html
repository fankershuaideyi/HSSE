Forbidden City

The construction of the Forbidden City of Beijing began in the fourth year of Yongle (1406) of the Emperor Chengzu of the Ming Dynasty. It was built based on the Nanjing Forbidden City. It was completed in the 18th year of Yongle (1420) and became the imperial palace of 24 emperors in the Ming and Qing dynasties. The National Day of the 14th year of the Republic of China (October 10, 1925) officially opened the Palace Museum. The Forbidden City of Beijing is 961 meters long from north to south, 753 meters wide from east to west, surrounded by a 10 meter high wall and a 52 meter wide moat outside. The Forbidden City has four gates, the Meridian Gate in the south, the Shenwu Gate in the north, the Donghua Gate in the east and the Xihua Gate in the west. Each of the four corners of the city wall has a charming turret. There is a folk saying that there are nine beams, eighteen columns and seventy-two ridges, which describes the complexity of its structure. [1] The buildings in the Forbidden City in Beijing are divided into two parts: the external court and the internal court.

The center of the Outer Court is the Hall of Supreme Harmony, the Hall of Central Harmony, and the Hall of Preserving Harmony, which are collectively called the Three Halls. The left and right wings of the Three Halls are supplemented by two groups of buildings, namely, the Wenhua Hall and the Wuying Hall. The center of the inner court is the Palace of Heavenly Purity, the Palace of Jiaotai and the Palace of Tranquil Tranquility, which are collectively called the Three Emperors. They are the main palaces where the emperor and empress live. After that, there is the Imperial Garden. The eastern and western six palaces are arranged on both sides of the rear three palaces, which are the places where the empresses and concubines live and rest. On the east side of the Eastern Six Palaces are Buddha hall buildings such as the Heavenly Palace, and on the west side of the Western Six Palaces are Buddha hall buildings such as the Main Hall. In addition to the Outer Court and the Inner Court, there are two parts of buildings: Outer East Road and Outer West Road. [1] The Forbidden City of Beijing is one of the largest and best preserved ancient wooden structures in the world. It is a national AAAAA tourist attraction. In 1961, it was listed as one of the first batch of key cultural relics under national protection; [4] It was listed as a world cultural heritage in 1987.
